Pressed Flower DIY Kit Guide

Kit Contents

  • Pressed Flower Sheets: Pre-pressed and dried flowers in various colors and shapes.
  • Tweezers: Precision tweezers for delicate flower placement.
  • Resin: Two-part clear epoxy resin for creating durable and beautiful crafts.
  • Mixing Tools: Small mixing cups and stir sticks for preparing the resin.
  • Mini UV Lamp: Compact UV lamp for curing the resin quickly and efficiently.
  • Clear Phone Case: A plain clear phone case to customize with the pressed flowers and resin.
  • Gloves: Protective gloves for safe handling of resin.
  • Instruction Manual: Step-by-step guide printed on the inside of the box.

Instructions

  1. Prepare Your Workspace

    Cover your work surface with the protective mat included in the kit. Lay out all the materials and tools.

  2. Select and Arrange Flowers

    Use the tweezers to carefully pick out the pressed flowers you want to use. Arrange them on the clear phone case until you're happy with the design.

  3. Mix the Resin

    In the mixing cup, combine the two parts of the epoxy resin as per the instructions on the bottles. Stir thoroughly with the stir stick until the mixture is clear.

  4. Apply the Resin

    Carefully pour the resin over your flower arrangement, ensuring it covers all the flowers and fills the case evenly. Use the stir stick to spread the resin if needed.

  5. Cure with UV Lamp

    Place the mini UV lamp over the resin-coated phone case. Cure the resin according to the lamp's instructions, usually for a few minutes.

  6. Final Touches

    Once the resin is fully cured and hard, inspect your phone case for any imperfections. Sand any rough edges if necessary. Your custom pressed flower phone case is now ready to use!

Tips and Tricks

  • Handle the flowers gently to avoid tearing or damaging them.
  • Experiment with different flower arrangements before applying resin.
  • Use the tweezers for precise placement of delicate flowers.
  • Ensure the resin is mixed thoroughly to avoid sticky spots.
  • Try other resin projects like bookmarks or coasters with leftover materials.
